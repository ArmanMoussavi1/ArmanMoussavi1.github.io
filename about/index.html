Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Northwestern University, where I am advised by Prof. Sinan Keten. I specialize in the theoretical and applied nanomechanics of engineering materials, with a focus on polymer-grafted nanoparticle systems, hydrogels, and gas-separation membranes.

My research combines large-scale molecular dynamics simulations, machine learning (including deep potential molecular dynamics — DeePMD), and continuum mechanics to unlock fundamental structure-property relationships in soft matter and nanocomposites. I aim to advance applications in aerospace, renewable energy, and sustainable infrastructure.

I earned my B.S. in Civil and Environmental Engineering from the University of Nebraska–Lincoln (2022), where I graduated with a 3.878 GPA and earned the Engineer in Training (EIT) certification. Prior to Northwestern, I conducted undergraduate research on bio-inspired roadside safety hardware and post-disaster structural reconnaissance using digital data sources.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to mentoring and teaching the next generation of engineers. I enjoy the challenge of translating cutting-edge nanoscale science into practical engineering solutions.

Short biography

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) under supervision of Matthew West. I also work with Luke Olson and Scott MacLachlan. The main focus of my Ph.D. has been optimizing partial differential equations solvers using machine learning. Along with my studies at UIUC, I have also done internships at NVIDIA and John Deere where I worked on machine learning projects, mainly concerning graph neural networks, computer vision, and reinforcement learning. I am originally from Iran and was born and raised in Tehran.
